Our Specials

Anda roti

"Anda" means egg, and "Roti" is a traditional flatbread made from wheat flour. Together, they form a protein-rich, satisfying dish that’s crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. Anda Roti is often enjoyed for breakfast, as a lunchbox filler, or as a late-night street snack.

chowmein

Chowmein is one of the most beloved fast food dishes in Nepal and across Asia. With its rich combination of stir-fried noodles, fresh vegetables, savory sauces, and optional meats or eggs, it’s a comfort food that satisfies every craving — whether you're looking for a quick lunch, a filling dinner, or a tasty street snack.

Momo

Momo is a mouthwatering dumpling dish that has become one of the most iconic and beloved foods in Nepal. Steamed or fried and filled with juicy ingredients, momos are a go-to snack or meal for people of all ages. Whether eaten on the street, at home, or in restaurants, momo is more than just food — it’s a part of Nepali culture.

Our Story

What makes us special isn’t just the facilities — it’s the people. From the aunties in the kitchen to the local youths at the reception, our hotel is powered by local hands and hearts.

Meet Our Chef

Five years ago, when Shrestha Khaja Ghar first opened its doors in a quiet corner of town, it was more than just a new eatery—it was a dream in the making. At the center of that dream stood Chef Mahesh Shrestha, a humble man with a big smile and even bigger passion for food.

From the very beginning, Chef Mahesh has been the soul of our kitchen. Whether it's the sizzling anda roti in the early morning, the spicy plate of chowmein during lunch, or the comforting steam of momo in the evening, every dish that comes from his hands carries a story, a memory, and a taste of home.
